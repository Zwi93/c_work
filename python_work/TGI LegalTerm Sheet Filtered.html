4        TRANSACTIONS

4.1           The parties agree that, pursuant to the Comprehensive Agreements:

4.1.1 The Landlord and the Tenant shall as soon as reasonably possible after the Signature Date irrevocably authorise the Company to act on its behalf and to manage the Deposit (“Appointment”).

4.1.2 The Company shall be authorised to invest the Deposit in a money market fund provided that such investment is compliant with the Rental Housing Act.

4.1.3 The appointment contemplated in this clause Error! Reference source not found. is irrevocable and may not be withdrawn once accepted by the Company.

4.1.4 The Company shall make use of the staff of the Fund to execute the functions contemplated in this clause Error! Reference source not found..

4.1.5 The Company may authorise the staff of the Fund to make withdrawals from the account into which the Landlord or the Tenant, as the case may be, has made payment in order to make the investment and pay any expenses.

4.1.6 The Lease Agreement shall record that the Tenant shall not be entitled to take occupation of the Premises until such time as the Deposit is paid to the Company and the Company has issued the Landlord and the Tenant with a reference number for the Deposit;

4.1.7 Neither the Landlord nor the Tenant shall be entitled to instruct the Company to withdraw the Deposit without the other’s prior written consent;

4.1.8 Within 7 (seven) days of occupation of the Premises by the Tenant, the Landlord and the Tenant shall inspect the Premises, take photograph of the Premises and forward such pictures of the Premises to the Company.  The Company will retain such images until the expiration of the Lease Agreement.  In the event of a dispute between the Landlord and Tenant, the Dispute Division shall be entitled to refer to these images while making its determination. In the event that the Landlord and Tenant fail to meet and inspect the Premises within 7 (seven) days of the Tenant’s occupation

of the Premises, the Tenant shall be entitled to photograph the premises and send copies of the photographs to both the Landlord and the Company.

4.1.9 The Tenant shall notify the Company, in writing, of the renewal, termination or expiration of the Lease Agreement by no later than 30 (thirty) days prior to the expiration of the lease period.  

4.1.10             In the event that the Tenant does not intend to renew the Lease Agreement and notifies

the Company of the termination or expiration of the Lease Agreement (“Tenant’s Notice”),  the Company shall issue a notice of the termination of the Appointment (“Termination Notice”) to both Landlord and Tenant, within [24 hours]  hours of receipt of the Tenant’s Notice.  The Termination Notice must be accepted or rejected by both Landlord and Tenant within 5 (five) days of date of Termination Notice.  In the event that the Tenant or the Landlord fails to notify the Company of its acceptance or rejection timeously, the Company will attempt to contact the Landlord and/or the Tenant telephonically and by way of electronic mail (“Company’s Follow Up”).  In the event that the Landlord and/or Tenant fails to respond to the Company’s Follow Up in writing, the Appointment shall be deemed to cancelled and the Deposit (or the balance thereof), together with any interest accrued thereon, will be paid to the Landlord or, where no amount are due and owing to the Landlord, the Tenant by no later than 30 (thirty) days following to the expiration of the Investment Mandate;

4.1.11  In the event that the Landlord or the Tenant rejects termination of the Appointment, the Landlord must notify the Company of the dispute which gave rise to the rejection and the dispute will be referred to the Dispute Division for mediation within 72 (seventy two) hours following the lodgement of the dispute.  In the event that the Dispute Division fails to reach a successful resolution of the dispute, same shall be referred to the rental tribunal of South Africa. The Deposit (or the balance thereof), together with any interest accrued thereon, shall be paid to the Landlord or, where no amount are due and owing to the Landlord, the Tenant within 3 (three) Business Days of resolution of the dispute.

4.1.12           If any amount due by the Tenant to the Landlord is not paid on the due date for payment thereof, then the Company shall pay the Landlord, any interest carried on such overdue amounts in terms of the Lease Agreement, on the last business day of the month in which the default occurs.  In the event that the Tenant fails to pay the overdue amounts, within 30 (thirty) days of date on which the payment was due, the Company shall terminate the Appointment and use the Deposit to pay any amounts owing by Tenant to the Landlord and pay the balance to the Tenant.  

4.1.13 In the event of the termination or expiration of the Lease Agreement, the Deposit (or the outstanding balance) thereon shall be paid to the Tenant unless the Lease Agreement is renewed, in which event the Landlord and Tenant will have an option to renew the Appointment.

4.1.14           the Maintenance Division shall:

4.1.14.1             attend to the maintenance of the Premises;

4.1.14.2 undertake day-to-day maintenance and minor repairs as required by Landlord and Tenant provided that the nature and extent of work does not justify outside contractors and the cost of work required is less than R20 000.00 (twenty thousand Rand); 

4.1.14.3             obtain three quotations for the necessary maintenance of the Premises; 

4.1.14.4 appoint any service companies as required, subject to the prior written approval of the Landlord and the Tenant in respect of such appointments.  Should the Landlord and Tenant fail to agree on the appointment of the relevant service provider, same shall be referred to the Dispute Division for determination.  This Dispute Division shall act as experts and not as arbitrators and their decision, determination or certificate shall be final and binding upon the parties affected thereby (save in the event of manifest error);

4.1.15 The Company will make available to the Landlord and Tenant all relevant invoices that indicate the costs incurred in the maintenance;

4.1.16           In consideration for the services provided, the Landlord shall pay the Company:

4.1.17    50 (fifty) basis points per annum, which amount shall be paid on each anniversary of the commencement date of the Lease Agreement against presentation of invoice;

4.1.18           R50.00 (fifty Rand) for each maintenance or repair job attended to by the Company

4.1.19 The Tenant shall bear the cost of R100.00 for preparation of the Investment Mandate and any renewal thereof.

9.3 Any party to the arbitration may appeal the decision of the arbitrator or arbitrators in terms of the AFSA rules for commercial arbitration.
